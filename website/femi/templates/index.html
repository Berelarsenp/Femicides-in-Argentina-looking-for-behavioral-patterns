Femicides in Argentina

Between January the 1st and December 31 of 2017, and according to Observatory of Violence Against Women "Not One [Woman] Less" ("Ni Una Menos") belonging to MuMaLá, a total of 298 femicides were registered in Argentina. This is a collaborative end-of-course project to put in practice our recently acquired knowledge on Data Science and Web Dev by showing the alarming reality of women an diverse people in Argentina.

The majority of the victims are young adults in an age range in which the society expects them to be mothers and/or professionals and/or independent

We can observe there are small but important linked cases that respond to children, relatives or friends of the target victim of the femicide, in order to torment and control her.

The great ascent and fall are due to the completeness of the data for those years. With that clarification, an increase is observed in the cases year by year.

There is a growing stream of cases towards the weekend. It can be related to an atypical moment of the week: the obligations decrease, there is more free time, more social activities, more money spent, etc.

Except La Pampa and San Luis, the average age is almost constant in the rest of the provinces.

It can be observed that women are the most killed, followed by a small number of men (linked cases like their childs or partners/lovers). Follow [this link](http://www.ign.gob.ar/NuestrasActividades/Geografia/DatosArgentina/DivisionPolitica) for demographic information.

CONCLUSIONS

To make a closure on the information displayed by the visualizations I’d like to add that although there is a real growth in violence against women, some specialists also say that more than an increase in cases, there is an increase in denunciations and greater focus on gender violence issues, which can be translated into datasets as a more cases. The patterns related to leasure time cought my attention and I think deeper research can be done about that.
